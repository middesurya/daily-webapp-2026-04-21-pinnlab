How PINNs Work

Physics-Informed Loss

Unlike standard neural networks, PINNs add a physics residual term to the loss function. The network must satisfy the PDE at random collocation points — enforcing physical laws without labeled data at those points.

Automatic Differentiation

TensorFlow.js computes exact derivatives ∂u/∂x, ∂²u/∂x² via the tf.grad() chain rule — the same derivatives that appear in the PDE. No finite differences needed.

The λ Balance

The physics weight λ controls the trade-off: too low and the network ignores physics (pure data fitting); too high and it struggles with the data term. Watch the loss decomposition bars to find the sweet spot.

Current PDE Info

The Heat Equation describes thermal diffusion. The initial temperature distribution smooths out over time as heat flows from hot to cold regions. Diffusivity α controls the speed.
